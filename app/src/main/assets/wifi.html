Connect Echo Dot to Wi-Fi

Before you begin

  • Plug your Echo Dot into a power outlet.
  • Open the Alexa app.

Echo Dot connects to dual-band Wi-Fi (2.4 GHz / 5 GHz) networks that use the 802.11a / b / g / n standard. Echo Dot does not connect to ad-hoc (or peer-to-peer) networks.

  1. In the Alexa app, open the left navigation panel and then select Settings.
  2. Select your device and then select Update Wi-Fi. If you're adding a new device to your account, select Set up a new device instead.
  3. On your device, press and hold the Action button until the light ring changes to orange. Your mobile device will then connect to your device. A list of available Wi-Fi networks appear in the app.
    Note: The Alexa app may ask you to manually connect your device to your device through your Wi-Fi settings.
  4. Select your Wi-Fi network and enter the network password (if required). If you don't see your Wi-Fi network, scroll down and select Add a Network (for hidden networks) or Rescan.
  5. Select Connect. After your device connects to your Wi-Fi network, a confirmation message appears in the app. You're now ready to use Alexa.
